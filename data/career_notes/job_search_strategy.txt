# Job Search Strategy Guide

## Overview

A successful job search combines online applications, networking, and personal branding. The most effective strategy uses multiple channels simultaneously, with heavy emphasis on building relationships that lead to referrals. Referrals are 5-10x more likely to result in interviews than cold applications.

## Core Concepts

### LinkedIn Optimization

#### Profile Essentials
- Professional headshot (clear, well-lit, friendly)
- Compelling headline with target role and key skills
- Detailed summary highlighting your value proposition
- Featured section with portfolio links and projects
- Skills section with endorsements
- Recommendations from colleagues and managers

#### Content Strategy
- Share industry insights and articles weekly
- Comment thoughtfully on posts in your field
- Write posts about your learning journey and projects
- Engage with recruiters and hiring managers
- Join and participate in relevant groups

### Networking

#### Building Relationships
- Attend industry meetups, conferences, and events
- Join online communities (Discord, Slack, Reddit)
- Participate in open source projects
- Connect with alumni from your university
- Conduct informational interviews with professionals

#### Informational Interviews
- Reach out to professionals in roles you aspire to
- Ask about their career path and daily work
- Seek advice on skills to develop
- Follow up with a thank-you note
- Maintain the relationship over time

### Referral Strategy

#### How to Get Referrals
- Identify contacts at target companies
- Ask for specific, actionable help
- Make it easy for them (send your resume and role details)
- Follow up once, then move on if no response
- Always send a thank-you note

#### Referral Etiquette
- Be specific about the role you are applying for
- Provide context about your qualifications
- Give them time to review before asking for action
- Never pressure or follow up too aggressively
- Express gratitude regardless of the outcome

### Application Tracking

#### Tracking System
- Use a spreadsheet or tool (Huntr, Teal)
- Track: Company, Role, Date Applied, Status, Contact
- Follow up 1-2 weeks after applying
- Review and adjust strategy monthly
- Track conversion rates at each stage

## Required Skills

### Professional Skills
1. Personal Branding - Presenting yourself consistently
2. Networking - Building and maintaining relationships
3. Communication - Clear, professional written and verbal
4. Research - Understanding companies and roles
5. Follow-through - Consistent, persistent effort

### Technical Skills
1. LinkedIn - Profile optimization and content creation
2. Application Tracking - Spreadsheet or CRM tools
3. Email Communication - Professional correspondence
4. Calendar Management - Scheduling interviews and follow-ups

## Common Mistakes

- Relying solely on online applications (the "spray and pray" approach)
- Not networking or building relationships before job searching
- Applying to roles without reading the full job description
- Ignoring follow-up after submitting applications
- Being too passive and waiting for recruiters to reach out
- Not researching company culture before interviewing

## Best Practices

- Start networking 3-6 months before you need a job
- Apply to roles where you meet 60-70% of the requirements
- Personalize every application with a tailored resume
- Follow up consistently but respectfully
- Track your metrics and adjust your approach
- Focus on building genuine relationships, not just transactional ones
