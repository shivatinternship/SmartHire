# ATS Resume Optimization Guide

## Overview

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are software tools used by employers to manage job applications. Over 95% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS. Your resume must be optimized for both the ATS and the human reviewer to maximize your chances of landing an interview.

## Core Concepts

### How ATS Works
- Scans resume for keywords matching job descriptions
- Parses contact information, work history, and education
- Ranks candidates based on keyword match percentage
- Stores resumes in a searchable database
- Filters out resumes that do not meet minimum criteria

### ATS Optimization Strategies

#### Keyword Matching
- Extract keywords from the job description
- Include exact-match keywords in your resume
- Use both spelled-out terms and abbreviations (e.g., "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)")
- Place keywords in context within bullet points, not just a keyword list
- Mirror the language used in the job posting

#### Resume Structure
- Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
- Avoid headers in footers (ATS may not read them)
- Use simple, clean formatting without tables or text boxes
- Save as PDF or DOCX (check job posting for preference)
- Use a professional, readable font (Arial, Calibri, Garamond)

#### Formatting Tips
- Consistent date format throughout
- Left-aligned text (avoid centering for body content)
- Standard bullet points (not custom symbols)
- No graphics, photos, or icons
- Single column layout (multi-column confuses some ATS)

## Required Skills

### Resume Writing
1. Quantify achievements with specific numbers
2. Start bullet points with strong action verbs
3. Tailor each resume to the specific job posting
4. Focus on results, not just responsibilities
5. Keep resume to 1-2 pages maximum

### Keyword Research
1. Analyze 3-5 job postings for your target role
2. Identify recurring skills and qualifications
3. Map your experience to those keywords
4. Include both technical and soft skill keywords
5. Use variations of important terms

## Common Mistakes

- Using a generic resume for every application
- Ignoring the job description keywords
- Including irrelevant work experience
- Using creative fonts or colorful formatting
- Submitting a resume that is too long (3+ pages)
- Not proofreading for spelling and grammar errors
- Using headers/footers for important information
- Including personal information (age, marital status, photo)

## Best Practices

- Customize your resume for each application
- Use the STAR method for bullet points (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Include a professional summary tailored to the role
- List skills in order of relevance to the job
- Add certifications and relevant training
- Include links to your portfolio or LinkedIn profile
- Test your resume by copying text from the PDF to verify parsing
