# Internship Search and Success Guide

## Overview

Internships are the most effective bridge between education and full-time employment. They provide hands-on experience, industry connections, and often lead to full-time offers. Starting your search early and applying strategically maximizes your chances of landing a quality internship.

## Core Concepts

### Internship Search Strategy

#### Timeline
- Start searching 3-6 months before the desired start date
- Fall internships: Apply August-October
- Summer internships: Apply August-December (big tech), January-March (startups)
- Spring internships: Apply October-November

#### Where to Apply
1. Company career pages (direct applications)
2. LinkedIn (follow companies, set job alerts)
3. Handshake (university-specific platform)
4. AngelList/Wellfound (startups)
5. Glassdoor and Indeed
6. Career fairs and networking events
7. University career services and alumni networks

#### Application Strategy
- Customize resume and cover letter for each role
- Apply to 50-100+ positions (competition is fierce)
- Track applications in a spreadsheet
- Follow up after 1-2 weeks if no response
- Apply to both large companies and startups

### Interview Preparation

#### Technical Interviews
- Practice coding on LeetCode, HackerRank, or CodeSignal
- Study data structures and algorithms fundamentals
- Do mock interviews with peers or platforms like Pramp
- Review the company's tech stack and prepare accordingly
- Prepare questions about the team and projects

#### Behavioral Interviews
- Prepare STAR stories for common questions
- Research the company's mission, values, and products
- Practice explaining your projects clearly
- Prepare thoughtful questions for the interviewer
- Show enthusiasm and genuine interest in the role

### Making the Most of Your Internship
- Set clear goals with your manager in the first week
- Ask questions and seek feedback regularly
- Document your accomplishments and impact
- Network with colleagues across teams
- Volunteer for challenging projects
- Request a return offer or referral before leaving

## Required Skills

### Job Search Skills
1. Resume Writing - Tailored, ATS-optimized resumes
2. Networking - Building professional relationships
3. Interview Preparation - Technical and behavioral
4. Follow-up - Thank you emails, status inquiries
5. Time Management - Managing multiple applications

### Professional Skills
1. Communication - Written and verbal
2. Time Management - Meeting deadlines and priorities
3. Teamwork - Collaborating effectively
4. Problem-solving - Taking initiative
5. Adaptability - Learning new tools quickly

## Common Mistakes

- Only applying to big tech companies (ignore startups and mid-size)
- Sending the same resume to every application
- Not preparing for technical interviews
- Skipping the follow-up after interviews
- Being passive during the internship
- Not networking during the internship

## Best Practices

- Start early and apply consistently
- Tailor each application to the specific role
- Build a strong online presence (LinkedIn, GitHub)
- Practice coding daily during interview preparation
- Send personalized thank-you emails after interviews
- Treat the internship as a 12-week job interview
